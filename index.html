Exploring Plant Intelligence and Urban Resilience

Biomimicry

  • Bee Orchid: Mimics bees to lure them into pseudocopulation.
  • Birds of Paradise: Pollinated by birds via structural adaptation.

Resilience Through Environmental Adaptation

  • Mangrove: Filters salt and stabilizes coastlines.
  • Acacia: Deters overgrazing and partners with ants.

Communication for Survival

  • Mycorrhizal Network: Trees and fungi exchange nutrients and signals.
  • Batflower: Mimics bats to attract pollinators.

Temperature Regulation and Seasonal Adaptation

  • Kale: Sweetens in cold through starch-to-sugar conversion.
  • Beet: Stores sugar to survive harsh conditions.

Movement for Protection

  • Mimosa: Closes leaves when touched; learns to ignore harmless stimuli.
  • Walking Onion: Self-replants through falling stalks.

Urban Heat and Resilience

  • Prickly Pear Cactus: Conserves water, reduces heat absorption.
  • Crape Myrtle: Heat-tolerant, with reflective bark and long flowering season.
